Rich and Creamy Tiramisu

This classic Italian tiramisu is a rich, velvety, no-bake dessert featuring layers of coffee-dipped ladyfingers and a luscious mascarpone cream.

It balances bold espresso with sweet, airy custard and bitter cocoa.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs, separated
  • 150g icing sugar (5⅓oz)
  • 300g mascarpone (10½oz)
  • 200ml strong espresso coffee (about 6 single shots)
  • 150ml Marsala DOC (about ⅔ cup)
  • 200g Savoiardi biscuits (7oz/about 24 pieces)
  • About 1 tablespoon Dutch-processed cocoa powder (8g/⅓oz)

Steps

  1. Whisk egg whites with half the sugar until firm peaks form. Set aside.
  2. Whisk egg yolks with remaining sugar until pale and creamy.
  3. Stir mascarpone to loosen it up, then fold it into the yolk mixture.
  4. Fold egg whites into this mixture and set aside.
  5. Combine coffee and Marsala.
  6. Dip half the biscuits into half the coffee mixture, so that they absorb it but aren’t soaked through, and arrange them in a single layer in a serving dish that’s about 26 x 21cm and 4–5cm deep (10″ x 8½″ and 1½–2″ deep).
  7. Top with half the mascarpone mixture.
  8. Dip remaining biscuits into remaining coffee mixture and arrange them in the dish.
  9. Top with remaining mascarpone mixture.
  10. Cover and refrigerate for at least 3 hours, preferably overnight.
  11. Dust with cocoa and serve your classic tiramisu.
